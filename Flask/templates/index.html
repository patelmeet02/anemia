Blood Parameter Details

Hemoglobin (Hb)

Normal Range: 12-16 g/dL (Women), 14-18 g/dL (Men)

What it measures: Hemoglobin is the iron-containing protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body.

Why it matters: Low hemoglobin levels are the primary indicator of anemia. When hemoglobin is low, the body doesn't get enough oxygen, leading to fatigue, weakness, and other symptoms.

Clinical significance: Hemoglobin levels below normal ranges indicate various types of anemia, including iron deficiency, vitamin B12 deficiency, or chronic disease-related anemia.

MCH - Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin

Normal Range: 27-31 pg

What it measures: MCH indicates the average amount of hemoglobin in each red blood cell.

Why it matters: MCH helps classify anemia types. Low MCH suggests microcytic anemia (small cells with less hemoglobin), while high MCH indicates macrocytic anemia (large cells with more hemoglobin).

Clinical significance: MCH is crucial for distinguishing between iron deficiency anemia (low MCH) and vitamin B12/folate deficiency anemia (high MCH).

MCHC - Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration

Normal Range: 32-36 g/dL

What it measures: MCHC shows the concentration of hemoglobin in red blood cells relative to their size.

Why it matters: MCHC helps identify the cause of anemia. Low MCHC indicates hypochromic anemia (pale cells), while normal MCHC suggests normochromic anemia.

Clinical significance: MCHC is particularly useful in diagnosing iron deficiency anemia, where cells become hypochromic due to insufficient iron for hemoglobin production.

MCV - Mean Corpuscular Volume

Normal Range: 80-100 fL

What it measures: MCV indicates the average size of red blood cells.

Why it matters: MCV is essential for anemia classification. Small cells (microcytic) suggest iron deficiency, while large cells (macrocytic) indicate vitamin B12 or folate deficiency.

Clinical significance: MCV helps differentiate between microcytic anemia (MCV < 80), normocytic anemia (MCV 80-100), and macrocytic anemia (MCV > 100).

Understanding Your Results

Normal Values: All parameters within normal ranges typically indicate healthy blood cell production and function.

Anemia Indicators: Low hemoglobin with abnormal MCH, MCHC, or MCV values helps identify the specific type and cause of anemia.
